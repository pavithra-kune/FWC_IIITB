\section*{Question}

If $X=1$ in the logic equation

\begin{align*}
\left[
X+Z
\left\{
\overline{Y}
+
\left(
\overline{Z}+X\overline{Y}
\right)
\right\}
\right]
\left[
\left\{
\overline{X}
+
\overline{Z}(X+Y)
\right\}
\right]
=1
\end{align*}

then

\begin{enumerate}

\item[(A)] $Y=Z$

\item[(B)] $Y=\overline{Z}$

\item[(C)] $Z=1$

\item[(D)] $Z=0$

\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section*{Solution}

Given:

\begin{align*}
X=1
\end{align*}

Substitute $X=1$ into the equation:

\begin{align*}
&
\left[
1+Z
\left\{
\overline{Y}
+
\left(
\overline{Z}+1\cdot\overline{Y}
\right)
\right\}
\right]
\\
&
\left[
\left\{
\overline{1}
+
\overline{Z}(1+Y)
\right\}
\right]
=1
\end{align*}

Since

\begin{align*}
1+A=1
\end{align*}

the first bracket becomes

\begin{align*}
1
\end{align*}

Now simplify the second bracket:

\begin{align*}
\overline{1}
+
\overline{Z}(1+Y)
\end{align*}


Since

\begin{align*}
\overline{1}=0
\end{align*}

and

\begin{align*}
1+Y=1
\end{align*}

we get

\begin{align*}
0+\overline{Z}(1)
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
\overline{Z}
\end{align*}

Therefore the equation becomes

\begin{align*}
1\cdot\overline{Z}=1
\end{align*}

Hence,

\begin{align*}
\overline{Z}=1
\end{align*}

So,

\begin{align*}
Z=0
\end{align*}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section*{Final Answer}

\begin{align*}
\boxed{Z=0}
\end{align*}

Therefore option \textbf{(D)} is correct.
